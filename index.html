Field Marshal Sam MANEKSHAW

BIOGRAPHY

SAM MANEKSHAW, in full Field Marshal Sam Hormusji Framji Jamshedji Manekshaw

Sam Manekshaw, affectionately known as Sam Bahadur, was born on April 3, 1914, in Amritsar, Punjab, India.

He served as the 8th Chief of the Army Staff of the Indian Army and played a pivotal role in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971.

Manekshaw's military journey included:

  • 1932: Commissioned into the Indian Military Academy
  • 1942: Military Cross for Bravery in World War II
  • 1961: Appointed as the General Officer Commanding-in-Chief, Eastern Command
  • 1969: Appointed as the 8th Chief of the Army Staff
  • 1971: Leadership during the Indo-Pakistani War, leading to the creation of Bangladesh
  • 1973: Conferred the Rank of Field Marshal, the first and only Indian Army officer to receive this honor

Field Marshal Sam Manekshaw's outstanding contributions to the Indian military and his leadership during critical moments in history have left an indelible mark. He passed away on June 27, 2008.




CAREER & AWARD

  • 1958: Joining DRDO[Defence Research and Development Organisation]
  • 1969: Satellite Launch Vehicle Program
  • 1980: Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP)
  • 1992: Director of DRDO
  • 1994: Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister
  • 1997: Publication of "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium"
  • 1997: Bharat Ratna
  • 2002: Presidency
  • 2006: Publication of "My Journey: Transforming Dreams into Actions"
